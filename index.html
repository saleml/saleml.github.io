My Contributions +

I build more capable and reliable AI. My research focuses on teaching machines to reason, plan, and generate diverse solutions under uncertainty.

My work is centered on three key areas:

  1. Developing Novel Methods: I am a core contributor to Generative Flow Networks (GFlowNets), a modern framework for structured probabilistic sampling. I also design new techniques for crucial problems like uncertainty estimation (DEUP) and efficient curriculum learning.
  2. Advancing Large Language Models: I apply these methods to improve the reasoning and alignment of LLMs, with projects focused on enhancing multi-step logic and fine-tuning models with human preferences.
  3. Building Tools for the Community: I believe in advancing science through shared tools. I created the torchgfn library for GFlowNets and have co-led the development of benchmarks like BabyAI, FinChain, and LLM-BabyBench.

Ultimately, I aim to create AI systems that are robust, creative, and trustworthy enough for real-world deployment.
